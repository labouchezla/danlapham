Barclays - Federated Organic Performance

Role: Executive Business Director (OMD UK)

Context: Financial services multi-business-unit requiring a unified and scalable approach to Organic Performance.

Challenge

Organic activity fragmented across business units, with inconsistent governance and limited visibility of impact. The rise of AI-driven search behaviour also created additional pressure to modernise measurement and content workflows.

Approach

Designed a federated SEO operating model supported by a central centre of excellence and stream-aligned teams. Strengthened governance, delivery rituals and strategic planning to ensure consistency and scale.

Impact

  • Increased investment in organic performance by 40% year on year, improved senior visibility, and established a long-term roadmap for BAU effectiveness and future search readiness.

Mercedes-Benz - Total Search integration

Role: Executive Business Director (OMD UK)

Context: Automotive brand seeking to unify performance channels to better capture demand across the funnel.

Challenge

Search and paid media teams operated in silos, with separate measurement frameworks and no shared planning cadence. This limited the brand’s ability to optimise against real user journeys and reduced the efficiency of spend and content.

  • Improved team alignment and cost efficiency, leading to increased investment in organic brand protection strategy and increased coverage of high-value terms, including 195K in additional organic traffic.

Mercedes-Benz Vans - Search cannibalisation solutions

Role: Executive Business Director (OMD UK)

Context: Automotive brand proposing a unique workaround in order to minimise traffic loss from post-migration inefficiencies.

Challenge

Post site migration, Mercedes-Benz Vans (US website) was capturing on average 18% of UK traffic due to a poor internationalisation strategy, leading to poor user experience and potential revenue loss. Because the US market was managed by a different team, on a different platform, it was not possible to impact the US source code directly.

Approach

Liaised with US counterparts to implement a workaround, instigating hreflang sitemaps specifically for UK and US markets, whilst simultaneously managing source code hreflang implementation for European markets.

Impact

  • £73.7K estimated savings in PPC spend annually and a 120% increase in UK traffic to the brand’s critical Sprinter page.

Clarks - Multi-market domain and CMS migration

Role: Executive Business Director (OMD UK)

Context: Fashion retailer seeking to consolidate digital real estate with a multi-market domain migration.

Challenge

Over a 17-month period, four domains as well as a CMS migration were moved into a single domain, putting at risk 40% of traffic (organic was Clarks’ single biggest traffic driver) and £45m revenue, where organic revenue was driven by 1.4m transactions.

Approach

Ensured all fundamental areas of SEO were optimised and redirect rules methodically put in place. Formed a unique risk register and response framework to minimise issues and delays and effectively manage more than 40 stakeholders. Built PPC integration into the project, ready to close any drops in search visibility.

Impact

  • 77% increase in Position 1 to 3 keywords, leading to a 49% increase in share of voice within weeks and a 50% increase in organic traffic.
  • European Search Award finalist for Best SEO Campaign 2023.

Context: Fashion retailer aiming to accelerate organic growth following site migration.

Future Media Group - AMP retirement strategy

Role: SEO Director (Blue Array)

Context: Large publisher seeking to modernise mobile architecture and reduce dependency on AMP (accelerated mobile pages).

Challenge

AMP had created technical debt, complexity in maintenance and inconsistent monetisation. Deprecating it carried risk to organic traffic and revenue, requiring a carefully staged strategy and performance modelling.

Approach

Audited AMP templates and created a phased rollout plan to mitigate risk. Modelled performance outcomes, validated technical requirements and provided change management support for internal teams.

Impact

  • Created a clear, low-risk deprecation pathway with a forecast revenue upside of approximately £1 million, supported by stronger long-term technical foundations.

IDP Connect - Clearing and app go-to-market momentum
