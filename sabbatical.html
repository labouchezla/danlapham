Professional Sabbatical (Planned)

January – February 2026

After completing my role at OMD UK at the end of 2025, I’m taking a short, intentional sabbatical before stepping into my next senior leadership role. This period is designed for perspective and renewal, while remaining close to how search straegy and decision-making are evolving in an AI-mediated landscape.

What this time is for

This sabbatical is a deliberate pause, not a departure.

  • Creating distance from day-to-day delivery to regain perspective after sustained leadership intensity
  • Reflecting on how discovery, strategy and operating models are changing as AI becomes embedded in decision-making
  • Returning refreshed, selective and ready to lead the next phase of growth

Staying strategically sharp

Alongside travel and rest, I’m selectively experimenting with AI as a thinking partner — focusing on how senior strategy and decision-making workflows may evolve, rather than execution or automation.

Frameworks & reflection

Using this time to consolidate thinking from the last few years of transformation work — focusing on what scales, what breaks, and what needs to change as discovery fragments.

Staying close to the landscape

Keeping a light-but-deliberate cadence with emerging changes in AI search, measurement, and operating models — prioritising what matters over what’s noisy.

Travel & perspective

This sabbatical includes time spent travelling in South America, Europe and Southeast Asia. Travel has always been an important way for me to reset perspective and return with renewed clarity and energy before the next chapter.

What comes next

I’ll be returning to the market in early 2026, focused on senior leadership roles where I can help organisations navigate an increasingly complex search and discovery landscape, build future-ready operating models, and drive sustainable growth through clarity, integration and strong strategic judgement.
